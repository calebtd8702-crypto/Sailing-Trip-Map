# Provisioning checklist

Scaled by passage length. Rule of thumb: water 4 L/person/day (plus 50% reserve), one hot meal a day minimum, and however much coffee you think you need — more.

## Every departure (day-hop or crossing)
- [ ] Water tanks topped + jerry cans full
- [ ] Fuel topped (main + dinghy) — log engine hours
- [ ] Weather checked (Helm passage strip + a second source)
- [ ] Float plan sent to someone ashore
- [ ] Fresh food for 2 days easy-grab
- [ ] Snacks/hydration reachable from the helm
- [ ] Seasickness meds taken BEFORE departure by anyone prone

## Coastal hop (< 3 days)
Everything above, plus:
- [ ] Ice / fridge cold-soaked
- [ ] 3 days fresh produce + protein
- [ ] Bread or tortillas (tortillas keep better)
- [ ] Pre-cooked first-night meal (nobody cooks on day one)
- [ ] Cash in local currency for the next stop

## Week-long leg
Everything above, plus:
- [ ] 7–10 days dry stores (rice, pasta, beans, oats)
- [ ] Canned protein (tuna, chicken, beans) 1/person/day
- [ ] Long-life produce: cabbage, carrots, onions, potatoes, citrus, green bananas
- [ ] UHT milk, eggs (unrefrigerated fine if never chilled — buy local)
- [ ] Propane/butane level checked
- [ ] Fishing gear rigged — fresh mahi beats anything in a can

## Ocean crossing (2+ weeks)
Everything above, plus:
- [ ] 30+ days food (crossing time × 1.5)
- [ ] Water: tanks + jerries + watermaker plan; ration math done
- [ ] Meals pre-planned by day; heavy-weather meals need zero cooking
- [ ] Treats/morale stash (chocolate hides well in the bilge — cool + dark)
- [ ] Vitamins; sprouting jar for fresh greens
- [ ] Spares audit against [gear inventory](gear-inventory.md)
- [ ] Med kit audit — antibiotics, painkillers, splint, suture/steri-strips
- [ ] Courtesy flags + Q flag for destination
- [ ] Destination checked in Helm country card (ports of entry, permits, what you can't bring in)
- [ ] Downloaded: offline charts, this repo, entertainment, weather GRIB plan
